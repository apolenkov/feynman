Three popular Linux distributions:

1. **Ubuntu** — Developed by Canonical, Ubuntu is one of the most widely used Linux distributions. It's known for being beginner-friendly, having a large community, and offering long-term support (LTS) releases. It's commonly used on desktops, servers, and in cloud environments.

2. **Fedora** — Sponsored by Red Hat, Fedora is a cutting-edge distribution that often introduces new features and technologies before they appear in other distros. It's popular among developers and is the upstream source for Red Hat Enterprise Linux (RHEL).

3. **Debian** — One of the oldest and most respected Linux distributions, Debian is known for its stability, strict adherence to free software principles, and massive software repository. It serves as the foundation for many other distros, including Ubuntu.
