A typical software engineering team hierarchy looks like this:

VP of Engineering
├── Director of Platform
│   ├── Engineering Manager (Platform)
│   │   ├── Senior SWE
│   │   ├── SWE
│   │   └── SWE
│   └── Engineering Manager (Infrastructure)
│       ├── Staff SWE
│       └── Senior SWE
└── Director of Product Engineering
    ├── Engineering Manager (Frontend)
    │   ├── Senior SWE
    │   └── SWE
    └── Engineering Manager (Backend)
        ├── Senior SWE
        └── SWE

The VP sets engineering direction and owns headcount. Directors manage 2-4 teams and translate strategy into roadmaps. Managers handle day-to-day execution, hiring, and performance. ICs (individual contributors) — Senior SWEs and SWEs — do the technical work.
